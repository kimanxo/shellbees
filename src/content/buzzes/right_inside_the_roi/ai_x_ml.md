## Key Concepts

- **Artificial Intelligence (AI):** The field of creating systems capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence.

- **Machine Learning (ML):** A branch of AI where models learn patterns from data instead of relying on explicit programming.

- **Deep Learning (DL):** A subset of Machine Learning that uses multi-layer neural networks to solve complex tasks.

- **Neural Network:** A mathematical model inspired by biological neurons that learns patterns through interconnected layers.

- **Transformer:** A neural network architecture based on the attention mechanism, powering modern Large Language Models.

- **Large Language Model (LLM):** A Transformer-based model trained on massive text datasets to understand and generate natural language.

- **Embedding:** A numerical vector representation of data that captures semantic meaning.

- **Attention Mechanism:** A technique that enables models to focus on the most relevant parts of the input when making predictions.

- **RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation):** A technique that combines information retrieval with language models to generate more accurate, knowledge-grounded responses.

- **Prompt Engineering:** The practice of designing prompts that guide AI systems toward producing more accurate and useful outputs.
